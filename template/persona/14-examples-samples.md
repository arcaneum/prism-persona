# 14 — Examples & Samples

> Annotated real writing samples across different contexts. This is the evidence dimension — it grounds the entire framework in your actual output rather than abstract descriptions.

---

## Guiding Questions

- What are your 5-10 best pieces of content that represent your voice?
- Can you identify pieces that show range (technical, personal, analytical)?
- What makes each sample representative of your authentic voice?

## Structure Guide

This file serves as an index pointing to the `samples/` directory. For each sample, include:

- **Title**: The piece name or a descriptive label
- **Context**: Where and when it was published, what prompted it
- **Demonstrates**: Which dimensions this sample illustrates (e.g., voice, emotional texture, philosophy)
- **Style Notes**: What specifically makes this piece "you"
- **File**: Relative path to the sample in `samples/`

Aim for range. Include your best technical writing, your most personal piece, your sharpest opinion, and your most polished public-facing content. The samples teach by example what the other 14 dimensions describe in the abstract.
