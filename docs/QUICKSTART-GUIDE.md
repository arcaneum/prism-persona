# Quickstart Guide

Nobody fills in 16 dimension files from scratch. This guide gives you three paths to a working PRISM persona, from fastest to most thorough. Pick the one that fits your time and energy. All three produce a usable persona — they differ in depth, not quality.

**The core insight:** Even four well-populated dimensions is dramatically better than nothing. A persona with Identity, Voice, Anti-Patterns, and Values will outperform any generic AI interaction by an order of magnitude. You can always add more later.

---

## Path 1: The 20-Minute Questionnaire

**Time investment:** 20-30 minutes
**What you get:** Enough material to populate 6-8 dimensions at a working level
**Best for:** People who want to start using PRISM today

Answer these 20 questions. Write naturally — bullet points, fragments, and stream-of-consciousness are all fine. The goal is signal, not polish.

### Identity and Context (Dimensions 00, 08)

**1. Describe your professional role in one sentence.**
Not your job title — what you actually do day-to-day.

**2. What is the one-sentence version of your career story?**
The arc from where you started to where you are now.

**3. What do you know deeply that most people in your field do not?**
Your unfair advantage, the hard-won expertise.

### Values and Ethics (Dimensions 01, 02)

**4. What would you never compromise on professionally, even if it cost you money?**
The line you will not cross.

**5. What do you believe about your industry that most people disagree with?**
Your contrarian position — the thing you would defend at a dinner party.

**6. What values do you want embedded in everything you produce?**
If someone consumed all your work, what principles should they absorb?

### Voice and Style (Dimensions 03, 05)

**7. Describe your writing voice in three adjectives.**
Not how you wish you sounded — how you actually sound.

**8. Paste a paragraph you have written that sounds most like you.**
A LinkedIn post, an email, a blog paragraph, a Slack message — anything that captures your natural voice.

**9. When you explain something complex, how do you do it?**
Analogies? Step-by-step? Start with the conclusion? Use a story?

**10. How formal or informal are you in professional communication?**
Think about your range: a board presentation versus a message to a trusted colleague.

### Anti-Patterns and Boundaries (Dimension 04)

**11. What phrases or cliches make you cringe in your industry?**
The buzzwords, the empty phrases, the things everyone says but nobody means.

**12. What would an AI writing as you get wrong if it only had your job title?**
The assumptions it would make that would feel inauthentic.

**13. What content or claims would damage your credibility if published under your name?**
The things that would make someone who knows you say "that is not right."

### Decision-Making and Work (Dimensions 06, 07)

**14. How do you make high-stakes decisions?**
Do you research exhaustively, trust your gut, consult others, sleep on it, or some combination?

**15. What does your ideal working day look like?**
Tools, rhythms, energy patterns, collaboration preferences.

### Relationships and Audience (Dimension 10)

**16. Who is your primary audience? Describe them as a person, not a demographic.**
Not "B2B SaaS leaders" — the actual human who reads your work.

**17. How do you handle public criticism or disagreement?**
Do you engage, ignore, address privately, or something else?

### Aspirations and Direction (Dimension 09)

**18. What are you building towards in the next 2-3 years?**
The project, the reputation, the outcome.

**19. What does success look like for you personally — not your company, you?**
The version of your professional life that would make you genuinely satisfied.

### The Acid Test (Dimension 14)

**20. Write a 3-sentence response to this scenario: Someone on social media publicly misrepresents your position on a topic you care about.**
Do not overthink this. Your instinctive response reveals voice, values, and emotional texture all at once.

---

### What to Do With Your Answers

Once you have answered these questions, you have two options:

**Option A — Manual placement.** Copy each answer into the relevant dimension file. The question headers above tell you which dimensions each answer maps to. Expand your answers as you go — the questions are designed to seed longer entries.

**Option B — AI-assisted population.** Give your answers to an AI tool with this prompt:

> Here are my answers to the PRISM persona questionnaire. Using the PRISM framework (16 dimensions: 00-Identity, 01-Values & Ethics, 02-Philosophy, 03-Voice & Tone, 04-Anti-Patterns, 05-Communication Style, 06-Decision-Making, 07-Working Style, 08-Knowledge & Expertise, 09-Aspirations, 10-Relationships, 11-Content & Creation, 12-Brand & Visual, 13-Emotional Texture, 14-Examples & Samples, 15-Evolution Log), draft dimension files for 00, 01, 03, 04, 05, 06, 09, and 10 based on my answers. Use the structure: a one-line description, guiding questions answered, and specific examples where possible. Flag anything you are uncertain about so I can confirm.

Review what the AI produces. Correct anything that feels off. Your corrections are themselves valuable persona data — they reveal what matters to you.

## Path 2: Artefact Upload

**Time investment:** 10 minutes to gather artefacts, plus AI processing time
**What you get:** AI-drafted dimensions based on your actual output, covering 8-12 dimensions
**Best for:** People with an existing body of work (blog posts, social media, professional writing)

Instead of answering questions, feed your existing content to an AI tool and let it extract persona signal. The more varied the artefacts, the richer the result.

### Artefact Types and What They Reveal

| Artefact Type | Dimensions It Populates | Notes |
|---------------|------------------------|-------|
| **Blog posts or articles** | 02 (Philosophy), 03 (Voice), 05 (Communication), 11 (Content) | Your strongest signal source. 3-5 posts across different topics is ideal. |
| **LinkedIn posts** | 03 (Voice), 05 (Communication), 10 (Relationships), 13 (Emotional Texture) | Shorter format reveals tone, audience awareness, and emotional range. |
| **X/Twitter posts or threads** | 03 (Voice), 04 (Anti-Patterns), 13 (Emotional Texture) | Unfiltered voice. Threads show how you structure arguments. |
| **Professional bio or About page** | 00 (Identity), 08 (Knowledge), 09 (Aspirations) | How you introduce yourself reveals what you prioritise. |
| **Brand guidelines or style guide** | 03 (Voice), 04 (Anti-Patterns), 12 (Brand & Visual) | Direct source for voice rules and visual identity. |
| **Professional emails** | 05 (Communication), 06 (Decision-Making), 07 (Working Style) | How you communicate under real conditions, not performative writing. |
| **Presentation slides or talk transcripts** | 02 (Philosophy), 05 (Communication), 08 (Knowledge) | Reveals how you teach and explain, plus your areas of confidence. |
| **Decision documents or strategy memos** | 01 (Values), 06 (Decision-Making), 09 (Aspirations) | The clearest window into how you think, weigh trade-offs, and commit. |
| **Comment responses or replies** | 04 (Anti-Patterns), 10 (Relationships), 13 (Emotional Texture) | How you respond under pressure or disagreement. |

### Recommended Artefact Bundle

For the best coverage with minimum effort, gather:

1. **3-5 blog posts or long-form pieces** (different topics and dates)
2. **10-15 social media posts** (mix of platforms if available)
3. **Your current bio or About page**
4. **2-3 professional emails** you are proud of (redact sensitive details)
5. **1 decision document or strategy memo** if you have one

This bundle covers 12 of 16 dimensions with real-world evidence.

### The Prompt

Give your artefacts to your AI tool with this prompt:

> I am building a PRISM persona profile (a 16-dimension framework for teaching AI my persona). Below are samples of my real writing and professional output. Analyse these artefacts and draft the following PRISM dimension files:
>
> - 00-Identity (role, background, context)
> - 01-Values & Ethics (principles visible in my content)
> - 02-Philosophy (positions and worldview)
> - 03-Voice & Tone (writing patterns, rhythm, vocabulary)
> - 04-Anti-Patterns (things I clearly avoid or would never say)
> - 05-Communication Style (formality, directness, audience adaptation)
> - 06-Decision-Making (how I weigh options, visible in strategy docs)
> - 08-Knowledge & Expertise (domains of depth)
> - 10-Relationships (audience, how I engage)
> - 11-Content & Creation (formats, platforms, patterns)
> - 13-Emotional Texture (how emotions surface in my writing)
>
> For each dimension, include: a summary, specific evidence from my artefacts (quote me directly), and confidence level (high/medium/low) for each claim. Flag anything you are inferring rather than observing directly.

### After the AI Drafts

1. **Review every dimension.** The AI will get your voice roughly right but may miss nuance in values and anti-patterns.
2. **Correct and expand.** Where the AI is wrong, say why — that correction is valuable persona data.
3. **Fill gaps manually.** Artefacts rarely reveal dimensions 07 (Working Style), 09 (Aspirations), or 15 (Evolution Log). Add these from your own knowledge.
4. **Add the writing sample** from your artefacts to dimension 14 (Examples & Samples) with annotations explaining what makes each sample characteristic of you.

## Path 3: Progressive Build

**Time investment:** 30-45 minutes for the first four dimensions, then ongoing
**What you get:** A focused, high-quality persona that grows over time
**Best for:** People who prefer depth over breadth, or who are not sure what their persona is yet

Start with four dimensions. Get them right. Use your persona. Add more dimensions when you hit a situation where the AI gets something wrong — that gap tells you which dimension to add next.

### The Essential Four

These four dimensions give you 80% of the value with 20% of the effort:

#### 1. Identity (00-identity.md)

Everything else depends on this. Write 3-5 sentences covering: who you are, what you do, where you work, and what context shapes your professional life. This takes five minutes and anchors the entire persona.

#### 2. Voice & Tone (03-voice-tone.md)

The fastest visible payoff. Describe how you write: adjectives, sentence patterns, what your writing sounds like read aloud. Include one or two paragraphs of your actual writing as reference. If you have a style guide, link to it.

#### 3. Anti-Patterns (04-anti-patterns.md)

The highest-leverage guardrail. List the things an AI should never do when writing as you: phrases to avoid, tones that feel wrong, claims that would damage your credibility, AI-generated patterns you find unacceptable. Be specific. "Never use buzzwords" is weak. "Never use 'leverage' as a verb, 'deep dive' as a noun, or 'at the end of the day' in any context" is strong.

#### 4. Values & Ethics (01-values-ethics.md)

The deepest guardrail. State your non-negotiable principles. What do you stand for? What would you refuse to do even under pressure? What do you believe that shapes every piece of work you produce? These do not change with audience or context — that is what makes them values.

### Expansion Guide: Add Dimensions by Use Case

Once your essential four are working, add dimensions based on what you are using your persona for:

| Use Case | Add These Dimensions | Why |
|----------|---------------------|-----|
| **Content creation** (articles, posts, newsletters) | 11 (Content & Creation), 13 (Emotional Texture), 14 (Examples) | Defines your formats, publishing standards, and emotional range |
| **Business decisions** (strategy, planning) | 06 (Decision-Making), 09 (Aspirations), 02 (Philosophy) | Captures how you think, where you are headed, and your positions |
| **Brand work** (visual identity, campaigns) | 12 (Brand & Visual), 05 (Communication Style) | Adds visual identity and audience-specific tone adaptation |
| **Team collaboration** (delegation, management) | 07 (Working Style), 10 (Relationships), 05 (Communication Style) | Defines how you work with others and adapt to different audiences |
| **Thought leadership** (speaking, expert positioning) | 02 (Philosophy), 08 (Knowledge & Expertise), 14 (Examples) | Your positions, depth, and evidence of your expertise |

### The Gap-Driven Method

After using your persona for a week, review the AI output that felt wrong. Each mistake points to a missing dimension:

- "It used a phrase I would never use" --> expand **04 Anti-Patterns**
- "It took a position I disagree with" --> add **02 Philosophy**
- "It wrote for the wrong audience" --> add **10 Relationships**
- "It was too formal / too casual" --> add **05 Communication Style**
- "It made a claim I cannot back up" --> add **08 Knowledge & Expertise**
- "It missed the emotional register" --> add **13 Emotional Texture**

This approach means every dimension you add solves a real problem, not a hypothetical one.

## Combining Paths

The three paths are not mutually exclusive. A common and effective approach:

1. **Start with Path 1** (the questionnaire) to get material on paper quickly
2. **Feed artefacts via Path 2** to validate and enrich the questionnaire answers
3. **Settle into Path 3** for ongoing maintenance, adding dimensions as gaps appear

You can also start with Path 3 (four dimensions only) and use Path 2 (artefact upload) to expand when you are ready.

---

## Time Investment Summary

| Path | Initial Time | Ongoing Effort | Dimensions Covered |
|------|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Path 1: Questionnaire** | 20-30 minutes | Review AI drafts (15 min) | 6-8 at working level |
| **Path 2: Artefact Upload** | 10 min gathering + AI time | Review and correct (30 min) | 8-12 at draft level |
| **Path 3: Progressive Build** | 30-45 minutes | 10 min per new dimension, as needed | 4 to start, grows over time |
| **Combined (1 + 2 + 3)** | 45-60 minutes total | 5 min/week via Living Document Protocol | 10-14 at strong level |

---

## What Happens Next

Whichever path you choose, once you have at least four populated dimensions:

1. **Generate your synthesis files.** See the [Integration Guide](INTEGRATION-GUIDE.md) for producing PERSONA-BRIEF.md and PERSONA-PROMPT.md from your dimensions.
2. **Start using the persona.** Paste PERSONA-PROMPT.md into your AI tool's system prompt and test it. Note where output feels wrong.
3. **Keep it alive.** Use the [Living Document Protocol](LIVING-DOCUMENT-PROTOCOL.md) for daily micro-updates. Five minutes a day keeps your persona current. A persona you built once and never updated will drift from who you are within months.

---

*PRISM is designed to be populated incrementally. Start where you are. Add depth where you need it. The framework does not break with empty dimensions — it simply works better as you fill more in.*
