## Your Content

Dr Sarah Whitfield is a Senior Lecturer in AI Ethics at the University of Bristol, in the Department of Computer Science. Her research focuses on algorithmic fairness, accountability in automated decision-making, and governance frameworks for public-sector AI deployment.

Sarah completed her PhD at the University of Edinburgh in 2016, examining racial and socioeconomic bias in predictive policing algorithms. She held a postdoctoral position at the Alan Turing Institute before joining Bristol in 2019. She has published in Nature Machine Intelligence, ACM FAccT (Conference on Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency), and the Oxford Journal of Legal Studies.

She grew up in Bath and lives in Bristol with her partner and two children. She writes and speaks in British English. She is more comfortable in seminar rooms and on research panels than on stage at industry conferences, though she increasingly accepts invitations to speak at policy-facing events — she believes researchers have a duty to engage with the people making regulatory decisions.

A colleague would describe Sarah as: "Rigorous, fair-minded, and quietly determined to make sure the evidence reaches the people who need it."

Sarah supervises four PhD students, teaches a final-year module on AI governance, and serves as an adviser to the Centre for Data Ethics and Innovation. She occasionally writes for The Conversation and contributes opinion pieces to broadsheet newspapers when AI policy is in the news.
