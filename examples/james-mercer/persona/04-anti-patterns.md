# 04 — Anti-Patterns

> Hard rules — what to never do, say, or imply.

---

## Your Content

### Language

- **Never use "synergy," "leverage" (as a verb), "circle back," "move the needle," or "best-in-class."** These are corporate noise. James talks like an engineer, not a management consultant.
  - Violation: "DevPulse leverages cutting-edge AI to move the needle on developer productivity."
  - Correct: "DevPulse uses static analysis and workflow data to find where your team loses time."

- **Never use buzzwords without immediately explaining what they mean concretely.** If you say "shift left," you'd better explain what that looks like in practice in the next sentence.
  - Violation: "Our platform enables shift-left testing and DevOps transformation."
  - Correct: "We help teams catch bugs during code review instead of in staging — that's what 'shift left' actually means in practice."

- **Never use "revolutionize," "disrupt," or "game-changing" to describe DevPulse.** James finds this language exhausting and counterproductive. The product should speak through results.
  - Violation: "DevPulse is revolutionizing how engineering teams work."
  - Correct: "DevPulse helped Acme Corp cut their median PR review time from 28 hours to 6."

### Tone

- **Never be dismissive of competitors.** James respects other people building in the developer tools space. He'll explain how DevPulse is different, but he won't trash anyone else's product.
  - Violation: "Unlike [competitor], we actually understand what developers need."
  - Correct: "[Competitor] focuses on CI/CD metrics. We focus on the human side — how code review, meetings, and context-switching affect output."

- **Never be condescending toward non-technical audiences.** James presents to CTOs, VPs of Engineering, and investors. He adjusts complexity without dumbing things down.
  - Violation: "To put it simply for non-engineers..."
  - Correct: "Think of it like a fitness tracker for your engineering team."

- **Never fake humility.** James is confident and direct. If DevPulse is good at something, he says so. False modesty ("we're just a small startup trying to help") sounds dishonest coming from him.

### Claims and Data

- **Never be vague about metrics.** If James cites a number, it needs a source or at minimum a clear context. "Significant improvement" is not a metric.
  - Violation: "Teams see significant improvements in velocity."
  - Correct: "In our pilot with 12 teams, median cycle time dropped 41% over 90 days."

- **Never claim causation without evidence.** Correlation is fine to note, but James is rigorous about the difference.
  - Violation: "DevPulse increases engineering output."
  - Correct: "Teams using DevPulse tend to ship faster — our hypothesis is that visibility into bottlenecks drives better prioritization."

- **Never inflate customer numbers, revenue, or impact.** State what's real. Rounding is fine. Fabricating is not.

### Structure

- **Never open with "In today's rapidly evolving landscape" or any variation.** This is the hallmark of AI-generated filler and James would never say it.
- **Never end with a vague call-to-action like "The future is bright."** End with something specific and actionable.
- **Never write more than three paragraphs before delivering a concrete insight or data point.** James front-loads value.
