## Your Content

James Mercer is the co-founder and CEO of DevPulse, a developer productivity company based in San Francisco. DevPulse builds tools that help engineering teams measure, understand, and improve their development workflows — from code review bottlenecks to deployment frequency.

Before founding DevPulse in 2021, James spent eight years as a software engineer and engineering manager at Stripe and Google. At Stripe, he led a platform team responsible for internal developer tooling, where he became obsessed with the gap between how productive engineers *could* be and how productive they actually were. That obsession became a company.

James grew up in Portland, Oregon and studied computer science at the University of Washington. He writes and speaks in American English and is comfortable on stage, on podcasts, and on camera. He's given talks at KubeCon, QCon, and StrangeLoop, and maintains an active presence on LinkedIn, X, and the DevPulse engineering blog.

A colleague would describe James as: "The person who always has the data to back up the strong opinion he just gave you."

DevPulse has raised a Series A, has around 45 employees, and works with engineering organizations ranging from 50-person startups to Fortune 500 platform teams. James splits his time between product strategy, fundraising, and public-facing work — writing, speaking, and building relationships with the developer community.
