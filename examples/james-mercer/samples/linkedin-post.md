# Sample: LinkedIn Post

> Platform: LinkedIn | Format: Short post | Topic: Developer productivity

---

We tracked 2,400 pull requests across 18 engineering teams last quarter. The single biggest predictor of shipping speed wasn't test coverage, CI pipeline time, or sprint planning quality.

It was how long PRs sat waiting for a first review.

Teams where the median time-to-first-review was under 4 hours shipped 2.3x more frequently than teams where it exceeded 24 hours. Same company. Same codebase. Same tools.

The bottleneck isn't your code. It's the gap between "ready for review" and someone actually looking at it.

Three things that moved that number for the fastest teams:

1. Review load balancing — no one reviewer getting crushed while others had empty queues
2. Smaller PRs by default — teams with a soft cap at 400 lines got faster reviews
3. Visibility — dashboards showing review wait times, updated daily

None of this is rocket science. But almost no one measures it, so almost no one fixes it.

If you want to ship faster, stop optimizing your pipeline and start optimizing your people's attention.
